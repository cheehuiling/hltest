Top 6 Hiking Trails in Penang

If street food and murals pop into your head at the mention of Penang, you’re gonna want to think again! The island is a treasure

trove for nature too, especially if you are a hiking enthusiast. Here’s where we suggest to go if you feel the itch to conquer

Penang’s hiking trails.

1. Bukit Hijau, Island Glades





Located opposite the peaks of the greater Penang Hill, Bukit Hijau is a lesser-known spot frequented by local hikers. Hike up

the Cangkat Tembaga Hiking Trail, a journey of roughly 4.2 kilometres, and be rewarded with a breathtaking view of the Farlim neighbourhood

and Penang Hill. There are several vantage points to view the island from the top and we'd recommend to come early in the morning to catch

beautiful sights of the sunrise.


To get to the trail head, you have to get to the Cangkat Tembaga road in Island Park. If you are heading along Jalan Masjid Negeri from

the Gelugor side, you should turn into the left junction (Jalan Tembaga) when you see The Penang Home For the Infirm and the Aged

(Old Folks Home) on the right side of the Jalan Masjid Negeri. Continue straight along Jalan Tembaga for about 600 meters and turn into

Lorong Tembaga. Once you are in Lorong Tembaga, go straight for about 60 meters and turn into Cangkat Tembaga. Drive till the end of this

road (about 300 meters) and you will see the trail head (a flight of stairs into the jungle) on the very end towards the left, just behind

the last house in the row. You can park your car here.

Before heading back, you may also visit the Bukit Hijau Waterfall. It is located side track at the trail head of Bukit Hijau.

At the trail head, turn right and walk in for about 5 minutes, you will reach the waterfall. The 50-minute hike is rated to be suitable

for even beginner hikers. The trail is kept clean and clear thanks to regular maintenance from the local community, and is dotted with

beautiful wild flowers as well as the stunning Bukit Hijau waterfall.

                                   
Duration (to and fro):1.5 hours
Level of Difficulty:Easy
Highlights:View of Air Itam, city view, best place for catching sunrise, waterfall

2. Gertak Sanggul Trail

This is a great hike at South West corner of Penang Island, crossing from Gertak Sanggul village to Pulau Betong area of Balik Pulau

through mountainous trail. This trail gives you a nice cross country feel and it just not lacks in exposing you to the great ions and fresh

air of forest nature.


It all starts from the serene Gertak Sanggul village where you can enjoy great sea views with beaches along the way. You can start from GPS

location 5.282094,100.192177 or any point along Jalan Gertak Sanggul. Park your car at any vacant area along the beach. Start walking towards

West. Just make sure beach is at your left side, not at your ride side to ensure you walk towards the right direction.


You will soon reach a small roundabout in the end of the road. You won’t miss those tall but crooked coconut trees at your left. Go further

down the road at 12 o’clock junction of roundabout, you will find the end of big tar road and smaller road going into the residential area. The

road starts to go slightly upwards and you will see houses at both sides.You will see this junction but just go straight. The small road will

start to turn into cracked cement trail and go into forest area. Continue walking forward and you will soon see this junction with clearance that

you can catch a glimpse of magnificent sea view. The Gertak Sanggul hiking trail is also a perfect place to catch the sunset!

                                   
Duration (to and fro):2 hours
Level of Difficulty:Easy
Highlights:Fruit farmland, sea view, beach, best place to catch the sunset

3. Bukit Cendana, Rainbow Rock

Bukit Cendana is a a peak which stands near but separate from the rest of the hills in the Penang Hill Range. Formerly known as

“Highlands”, Bukit Cendana’s history spans back to the mid 1800s when it used to be the site of a retreat whose ruins are still visible

at its peak. Today, its slopes are home to popular trails like the Station 3,5 and 39.


There are a number of trails up to Bukit Cendana, but this guide describes the one which starts from the popular Moon Gate trail. If you

are heading in the direction of the Penang Botanic Gardens, The Moon Gate trail starts to the left of Jalan Kebun Bunga about 400 meters before

the Botanic Gardens. Its circular entrance painted in a bright coat of yellow is never too difficult to spot as you pass this road.


Follow the popular trail up from Moon Gate to Station 5. It will pass through a wide and eroded jungle trail, wind its way up a series of steps

with occasional views of townships below and finally come to a flat wide path. It will take you about 20-25 minutes to reach this wide flat path.

A further 5 minutes of walking along this wide path will bring you to the Station 5 rest area. This rest area built on the donations and goodwill of

generations of hikers is a popular hiking spot in Penang. Take a short rest here before continuing.


Just after the Station 5 rest area, the main trail goes straight along the contour of the hill, joining up to the Jeep Track at Station 84. However,

you’ll notice that just before the makeshift badminton court and after the Gym Equipment in Station 5, a series of steps goes off to the right on the

slope. Take this right turn and continue up the slope. In about 50 meters of walking, you’ll come to a junction where there is a left fork up the slope

and another which just keeps going straight. Take the left fork and continue up the slope. This trail zigzags along the edges of the Highlands Permanent

Forest Reserve and up the Bukit Cendana slope. In about 50 minutes from Station 5, you will be standing at the peak of Bukit Cendana.

                                   
Duration (to and fro):2.5 hours
Level of Difficulty:Moderate
Highlights:Cityscape view, fruit farmland, honey cultivation centre

4. Iconic Hill aka Botak Hill

Iconic Hill or previously known as Botak Hill become a new hiking spot at Bukit Gambir, Penang. The route is just cement with a very flat

surface. Hence, the environment is spacious and a lot of cyclists too.


If you want to enjoy the epic sunrise, I would suggest reaching Beverly Heights around 6-6.30am. This timing confirms that will have ample times

to witness the sun rising from the sea at the Iconic Hilltop. Don't worry about you will getting lost as the crowd of hikers are always in a big group,

you can just follow them to the top of the hill.


As always, I suggest can come down from the Iconic Hill as early as around 8am. The later you walk down, the sun is getting hotter and you no longer

enjoy the journey. I would say this location is suitable for family as it's beginner-friendly and nothing challenging. At the top of the Iconic Hill,

you can see both Penang Bridges, Kek Lok Si, Paya Terubang and a lot of hills. It always feels refreshing when you can view various part of the Penang

with a higher attitude.

                                   
Duration (to and fro):2 hours
Level of Difficulty:Easy. Cement route with a flat surface
Highlights:View of Penang Island, Penang Bridge, Kek Lok Si, Paya Terubang and a lot
of hills. Also the best place to catch sunrise

5. Pantai Keracut (Penang National Park)

Penang National Park in Teluk Bahang have a few hiking trails, the one to Monkey Beach and Muka Head lighthouse, Pantai Kerachut

(Turtle Beach) to Teluk Kampi, and the newest one, the trail to Bukit Batu Hitam. All trails are well signposted and easy to follow,

and it all begin at the entrance to the National Park, where you are required to sign in at the entrance of the park. Entrance to the park

is free of charge.


Before you set out, decide which trail you want to hike, and whether you want to hike there and back, or to arrange for a fishing boat to

pick you up at one of the beaches at a specific time. There are desks just outside the National Park where you can do this, and the price is

around RM100 for the whole boat which can take around 10 people. Once you enter the park, you will walk along a flat brick pathway beside

the beach, after crossing a bridge, a signpost clearly marks the beginning of the trails.


There are two paths through the rainforest to Pantai Kerachut. The first and easiest way is to walk for 1/2 km along the coast from the park

entrance and take the first left up over the hill and down again the other side - This pathway is quite wide, easy to follow and not too steep. The

other slightly longer way is to head to the Canopy Walkway and continue up the hill from there where it meets the first path near the top of the hill.

This path is a lot narrower, steeper, less used and with much more forest debris littered over it making it harder to walk along. On the way down from

the top of the hill, there is also another split in the path. If you keep going straight this will take you to a bridge over the meromictic lake at the

end of the beach, turning left will take you around the lake to the campsite.


The beach is known for its meromictic lake (a mix of fresh and sea water) and the small turtle sanctuary at the far end of the beach, which was set

up in 1995 to collect the recently hatched young of the Green Turtles that lay their eggs on the beach, and release them back into the wild once they

are old enough to fend for themselves with a lower risk of predation.

                                   
Duration (to and fro):3 hours
Level of Difficulty:Moderate. The route to Kerachut Beach is steeper and has more adventurous
pathways compared to Monkey Beach
Highlights:Forest reserve, Monkey Beach, Kerachut Beach, Muka Head Lighthouse

6. Cherok Tokun Hill, Bukit Mertajam

Cherok Tokun is a 6.8 kilometer moderately trafficked loop trail located near Bukit Mertajam, Penang, Malaysia that features beautiful wild flowers

and is rated as moderate. The trail is primarily used for hiking, walking, nature trips, and mountain biking and is accessible year-round.Cherok Tokun

is Malay village and subdistricts of Bukit Mertajam. It is located to the east of Bukit Mertajam town centre, to the north of Alma and to the west of

Machang Bubok.


You can hike up Bukit Tokun either through a tarred road, or a jungle track. If you are a beginner at hiking up a hill, or alone, it is advisable to

use the tarred road. Only use the jungle track if you are there with a group of people, and are “confident”. The hike takes about 1 hour to reach the

summit.


Along the way, you will be surrounded by trees, and have the chance to enjoy nature. You will likely see monkeys, big ants, and milipedes as

you hike up. There are also many rest areas along the tarred road. At the top of Bukit Tokun are communication towers, which is the point where you

have to turn back and hike down.
                                   
Duration (to and fro):3 hours
Level of Difficulty:Moderate
Highlights:Recreational Park, Tea House, Flora and Fauna, Full view of Bukit Mertajam
town and Mengkuang Dam at the peak
